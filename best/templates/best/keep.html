1

Wash fresh Gamgyuls in salt water first. After drying the washed fruits completely, stacks them up in a box covered with newspaper.

2

When you store it on top of each other, it will easily go bad, so put a paper towel under it and keep it apart with the top of the tangerine facing down.

3

If the amount is large, you can store them for a relatively long time by putting newpapers between gamgyuls.

4

Store in a well-ventilated, cool, shady place of 10℃ or in a refrigerator that can maintain a temperature of 3~5℃.

5

If the humidity is too high, decomposition is likely to occur due to green mold, so ventilation is required, however, when it is too dry, the fresh taste will disappear quickly, so cover it with newspaper to maintain proper moisture.
